# Advisory Board: Customer Experience Overview

> **Date:** 2026-04-18
> **Presenter:** Derek (Developer)
> **Topic:** What the Hy-lee website delivers for our customers

## The Core Principle

Everything we've built is designed around one question: what does a customer need to feel confident enough to buy, and valued enough to come back?

Our target customer is living or aspiring to live in a small space — tiny homes, RVs, micro apartments, dorms, boats. They're not browsing casually. They have specific constraints, specific needs, and limited tolerance for a shopping experience that wastes their time. Every decision we made in building this website comes back to that reality.

---

## What Our Website Does for Customers

### 1. Makes It Easy to Find the Right Product

Customers shopping for small-space living have specific needs. Our website helps them get to the right product quickly — through smart search, category browsing, and filters that show exactly what they've applied and let them adjust without starting over. The goal is zero frustration between landing on the site and finding what they're looking for.

**How this plays out in practice:**

- **Search** works from anywhere on the site — results appear instantly as a customer types, surfacing product names, categories, and direct matches so they're never stuck hunting.
- **Category browsing** is structured for how small-space customers actually think. Top-level categories (furniture, appliances, storage, etc.) break into subcategories that reflect their use case, not a warehouse taxonomy.
- **Filters are transparent.** When a customer applies a filter — price, size, type — they see exactly what's active as labeled chips above the results. Removing one filter doesn't reset everything else. They stay in control.
- **Sort options** include newest arrivals, so customers who return to the site know immediately what's changed since their last visit.

The result is a path from "I need something" to "I found it" that takes minutes, not frustrating dead-end sessions.

---

### 2. Gives Them the Information to Say Yes

Once a customer finds a product, the page is designed to answer every question before they have to ask — photos, dimensions, specs, and real reviews from other customers. For furniture and appliances specifically, there's a built-in fit-check feature so customers can confirm a product works for their space before buying. The result is fewer hesitations, fewer returns, and more confident purchases.

**How this plays out in practice:**

- **Photography** is presented in a gallery format customers can browse — multiple angles, lifestyle shots, and close-ups of materials and hardware. Small-space buyers are buying with precision, not impulse.
- **Specifications** are presented in two tiers: the six most decision-relevant specs appear immediately (dimensions, weight, capacity, etc.), with the full technical sheet available below for customers who need it.
- **The "Does It Fit" tool** is available on furniture and appliance listings. A customer can enter their room dimensions and get an immediate confirmation whether the product works in their space — before they add anything to cart. This directly reduces buyer's remorse and return rates on our two highest-margin categories.
- **Real customer reviews** — sourced and displayed directly on the product page — give social validation that no amount of copy can replicate. Customers trust other customers.
- **Warranty and return information** is surfaced directly on the product page, not buried in a footer. A customer shouldn't have to go looking for "what happens if this doesn't work out" — that question should already be answered before they buy.

The goal is that by the time a customer reaches the Add to Cart button, they have no unanswered questions.

---

### 3. Gets Them Through Checkout Without Friction

Checkout is where most online stores lose customers. Ours walks them through the process step by step, keeps things simple (one shipping option, no re-entering information), and gives them a full order review before they commit. A clear confirmation page closes the loop. Less friction at this stage directly improves conversion.

**How this plays out in practice:**

- **Step-by-step flow** breaks checkout into four clear stages: cart review, shipping, payment, and order review. Customers always know where they are and what comes next. There's no ambiguity about whether something was saved or submitted.
- **No redundant data entry.** Information entered at one step carries forward. A customer who enters their shipping address doesn't re-enter it for billing if they're the same.
- **Simplified shipping** means customers aren't paralyzed by a shipping options matrix. Standard delivery is presented clearly with an expected window — a deliberate decision that reduces decision fatigue and drop-off.
- **Full order review before purchase.** Before anything is charged, the customer sees their complete order — items, address, payment method, and total — with the ability to go back and edit any section. They commit with confidence, not hope.
- **Confirmation closes the loop.** After purchase, a confirmation page (and email) clearly summarizes what was ordered, when it's expected, and what to do if anything is wrong. Customers who feel informed after a purchase are more likely to return.

Every one of these design decisions exists because data consistently shows that uncertainty kills conversions. We removed the uncertainty.

---

### 4. Builds a Relationship, Not Just a Transaction

Customers with an account can see their full order history, manage their wishlist and addresses, and track active orders — all in one place. Paired with our promotional tier structure (first purchase, account creation, referrals, partner codes), we have built-in reasons for customers to engage more deeply over time. Stackable discounts reward loyalty without eroding margin on already-discounted products.

**How this plays out in practice:**

- **The account dashboard** is a personal hub — not a sparse order list. Customers see recent orders, saved items, addresses on file, and active promotions available to them. It's designed to feel like a place worth returning to.
- **Wishlist** lets customers save products they're considering without committing. For small-space buyers who are planning a move or a renovation over weeks or months, this is a meaningful feature — it keeps them connected to our product catalog between purchase decisions.
- **Order history and tracking** gives customers visibility into everything they've bought and where active shipments stand. No hunting through email inboxes.
- **Promotional tiers** are a structured loyalty engine:
  - 10% off on first purchase — low barrier to convert a new visitor
  - 15% off for creating an account — incentivizes the relationship
  - 20% off for referring a friend — turns satisfied customers into our acquisition channel
  - 25% partner codes — for brand partnerships and affiliates
- **Discounts are stackable** where eligible, which means a customer who creates an account and refers a friend gets compound value — not a choice between benefits.
- **Already-discounted products are excluded** from promo stacking, which protects margin on sale inventory while keeping the program generous everywhere else.

The promotional structure isn't just a discount program — it's a mechanism for turning one-time buyers into repeat customers and repeat customers into advocates.

---

### 5. Stands Behind Every Sale

Returns are handled through a guided process where customers choose their resolution — exchange, replacement, store credit, or refund. The experience is designed to feel supportive, not adversarial. Customers who have a smooth return are more likely to buy again than customers who never returned anything. Our policies (return policy, FAQ) are clearly written and easy to find, which reduces anxiety before and after purchase.

**How this plays out in practice:**

- **The return process is guided**, not a form customers submit and then wait on. It walks them through selecting which items they're returning, why, and what resolution they want — exchange, replacement, store credit, or refund. They leave the process knowing what happens next.
- **Resolution options put customers in control.** Rather than deciding for them how their return is resolved, we let them choose. This meaningfully reduces the feeling that a return is a loss — and increases the likelihood they stay in our ecosystem (store credit, exchange) rather than walking away.
- **15-day return window** is clearly stated upfront, with a 24-hour window specifically for damaged goods reported on delivery. Customers know the rules before they buy.
- **7–14 day refund processing** is communicated explicitly — no vague "allow 5–10 business days." Clear timelines reduce follow-up inquiries and reduce the anxiety gap between return initiation and money back.
- **Free return shipping for damaged items** removes the financial sting from the worst-case scenario. If something arrived broken through no fault of the customer, they shouldn't pay to send it back.
- **FAQ and return policy pages** are written in plain language, easy to find from the footer, and structured so customers can scan for the answer they need without reading everything. A customer who can answer their own question before purchase is a customer who buys with confidence.

The underlying belief here is that how a business handles the moment a customer is unhappy determines whether that customer ever comes back. We've built the return experience to be the part of our business that surprises people.

---

### 6. Ready to Grow With Us

The website is built to support multiple languages and scale without rebuilding from scratch. As the customer base grows, we can expand into new markets without significant additional development investment.

**How this plays out in practice:**

- **Multi-language support** is live. English, French, and Spanish are fully translated — not just interface labels, but product categories, policies, FAQs, and marketing copy. A French-speaking customer gets a complete experience, not a patchwork one.
- **All content is locale-driven**, meaning new translations or content updates across all languages happen in one place, not across dozens of individual page files. Adding a fourth language is a content task, not a development project.
- **The platform scales horizontally.** Adding new product categories, new promotional structures, or new customer account features doesn't require rebuilding core infrastructure. We've designed for extension from the start.
- **International markets** are a viable next step without re-platforming. The groundwork — multi-language, flexible checkout, account infrastructure — is already in place.

Growth that requires proportional development cost is growth with a ceiling. We've built without that ceiling.
